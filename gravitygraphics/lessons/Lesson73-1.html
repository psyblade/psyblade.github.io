Lesson 73-1: Planetary Movements

In this code, you're working with basic trigonometry and geometry to simulate the movement of a dot around a circle. This exercise teaches a few key mathematical and programming concepts:

The dot is moving in a circular path, with its position calculated using trigonometric functions: cosine and sine. 

Base length=∣r⋅* cos(θ)∣
Vertical length =∣r⋅* sin(θ)∣

The angle (θ) is measured in radians, not degrees.

360 degrees = 2π radians
